**Question 1:** For order matching, the best buy order is the one with the:

**Options:**

A. Lowest price
B. Highest quantity
C. Highest price
D. Earliest time stamp

**Answer:** Highest price

**Explanation:** The system wants to match sellers with the highest possible prices offered by buyers, so the best buy order is the one with the highest bid price.

---

**Question 2:** For order matching, the best sell order is the one with the:

**Options:**

A. Highest price
B. Highest quantity
C. Lowest price
D. Latest time stamp

**Answer:** Lowest price

**Explanation:** The system wants to match buyers with the lowest possible prices offered by sellers, so the best sell order is the one with the lowest ask price.

**Question 9:** If the put open interest exceeds the call open interest (PCR > 1), it is typically taken as a:

**Options:**

A. Bearish signal
B. Bullish signal
C. Neutral signal
D. High volatility signal

**Answer:** Bullish signal

**Explanation:** A high PCR can suggest that more participants are betting on a price decline (buying puts) or selling puts, which could be interpreted as a bullish signal (selling puts implies expecting prices not to fall significantly).

**Question 14:** A long straddle strategy is undertaken when the trader expects:

A. Rs. 202.5
B. Rs. 225
C. Rs. 150
D. Rs. 212.5

**Question 55:** [Ref. Question] A trader creates a Long Straddle by buying a Call option at strike Rs. 15100 (premium Rs. 121) and a Put option at the same strike Rs. 15100 (premium Rs. 91). Calculate the upper and lower break-even points of this strategy.

**Options:**

A. Upper BE: Rs. 15312 | Lower BE: Rs. 14888
B. Upper BE: Rs. 15221 | Lower BE: Rs. 15009
C. Upper BE: Rs. 15362 | Lower BE: Rs. 14838
D. Upper BE: Rs. 15100 | Lower BE: Rs. 14888

**Answer:** A. Upper BE: Rs. 15312 | Lower BE: Rs. 14888

**Explanation:** Total Premium Paid = Call Premium + Put Premium = 121 + 91 = Rs. 212. Upper Break-even = Strike + Total Premium = 15100 + 212 = Rs. 15312. Lower Break-even = Strike - Total Premium = 15100 - 212 = Rs. 14888.
