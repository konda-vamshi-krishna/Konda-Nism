**Question 1:** Case: An index comprises three stocks: Stock A, Stock B, and Stock C. The base value of the index was set to 100 on January 1, 2024. On April 1, 2024, the prices changed to: Stock A: Rs. 120, Stock B: Rs. 210, Stock C: Rs. 160. The index is a market capitalization-weighted index. Calculate the market capitalization of the index on January 1, 2024.

**Options:**

A. Rs. 11,400 lakhs
B. Rs. 10,500 lakhs
C. Rs. 17,000 lakhs
D. Rs. 15,600 lakhs

**Answer:** C. Rs. 17,000 lakhs

**Explanation:** (100 * 50) + (200 * 30) + (150 * 40) = 5000 + 6000 + 6000 = Rs. 17,000 lakhs.

**Question 10:** Case: The spot price of Stock B is Rs. 100. A one-month futures contract on Stock B is trading at Rs. 90. The contract multiplier is 200 shares. The arbitrageur can invest proceeds from cash market sales at 9% p.a. for one month. If the arbitrageur carries the position for one month, what is the total gain from this arbitrage, including interest earned, assuming continuous compounding?

**Options:**

A. Rs. 2,000
B. Rs. 2,150.56
C. Rs. 2,300
D. Rs. 2,450.56

**Answer:** B. Rs. 2,150.56

**Explanation:** Immediate gain = 200 * 10 = Rs. 2,000. Interest earned on Rs. 20,000 (100 * 200 shares) at 9% p.a. for one month (1/12 years) continuously compounded = 20,000 * (e^(0.09 * 1/12) - 1) = 20,000 * (e^0.0075 - 1) = 20,000 * (1.007528 - 1) = 20,000 * 0.007528 = Rs. 150.56. Total gain = 2,000 + 150.56 = Rs. 2,150.56.
