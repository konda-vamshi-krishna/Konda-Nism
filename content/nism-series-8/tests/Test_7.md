**Question 1:** Case: An index comprises three stocks: Stock A, Stock B, and Stock C. On January 1, 2024, their prices and shares outstanding were: Stock A: Rs. 100 (50 lakh shares), Stock B: Rs. 200 (30 lakh shares), Stock C: Rs. 150 (40 lakh shares). The base value of the index was set to 100 on January 1, 2024. On April 1, 2024, the prices changed to: Stock A: Rs. 120, Stock B: Rs. 210, Stock C: Rs. 160. The index is a market capitalization-weighted index. Calculate the market capitalization of the index on January 1, 2024.

**Options:**

A) Rs. 11,400 lakhs
B) Rs. 10,500 lakhs
C) Rs. 17,000 lakhs
D) Rs. 15,600 lakhs

===
**Answer:** C) Rs. 17,000 lakhs

**Explanation:** (100 * 50) + (200 * 30) + (150 * 40) = 5000 + 6000 + 6000 = Rs. 17,000 lakhs.

C) Rs. 30,000

**Question 10:** Case: The spot price of Stock B is Rs. 100. A one-month futures contract on Stock B is trading at Rs. 90. The contract multiplier is 200 shares. The arbitrageur can invest proceeds from cash market sales at 9% p.a. for one month. If the arbitrageur carries the position for one month, what is the total gain from this arbitrage, including interest earned, assuming continuous compounding?

**Options:**

A) Rs. 2,000
B) Rs. 2,150.56
C) Rs. 2,300
D) Rs. 2,450.56

===
**Answer:** B) Rs. 2,150.56

**Explanation:** Immediate gain = 200 * 10 = Rs. 2,000. Interest earned on Rs. 20,000 (100 * 200 shares) at 9% p.a. for one month (1/12 years) continuously compounded = 20,000 * (e^(0.09 * 1/12) - 1) = 20,000 * (e^0.0075 - 1) = 20,000 * (1.007528 - 1) = 20,000 * 0.007528 = Rs. 150.56. Total gain = 2,000 + 150.56 = Rs. 2,150.56.

The investment proportions (weights) in the portfolio are 40% for Stock X, 30% for Stock Y, and 30% for Stock Z. The market value of the portfolio is Rs. 1,00,00,000. The index futures price is 20,000, and the lot size is 50. How many index futures contracts are required to hedge this portfolio's systematic risk (rounded to the nearest whole number)?

**Options:**

A) 8 contracts
B) 9 contracts
C) 10 contracts
D) 11 contracts

===
**Answer:** D) 11 contracts

**Explanation:** Number of contracts = (Value of Portfolio * Portfolio Beta) / (Index Futures Level * Contract Multiplier) = (1,00,00,000 * 1.07) / (20,000 * 50) = 1,07,00,000 / 10,00,000 = 10.7 contracts. Rounded to the nearest whole number, this is 11 contracts.
