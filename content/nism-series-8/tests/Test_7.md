**Question 1:** Case: An index comprises three stocks: Stock A, Stock B, and Stock C. On January 1, 2024, their prices and shares outstanding were: Stock A: Rs. 100 (50 lakh shares), Stock B: Rs. 200 (30 lakh shares), Stock C: Rs. 150 (40 lakh shares). The base value of the index was set to 100 on January 1, 2024. On April 1, 2024, the prices changed to: Stock A: Rs. 120, Stock B: Rs. 210, Stock C: Rs. 160. The index is a market capitalization-weighted index. Calculate the market capitalization of the index on January 1, 2024.

**Options:**

A) Rs. 11,400 lakhs
B) Rs. 10,500 lakhs
C) Rs. 17,000 lakhs
D) Rs. 15,600 lakhs

**Answer:** Rs. 17,000 lakhs

**Explanation:** (100 * 50) + (200 * 30) + (150 * 40) = 5000 + 6000 + 6000 = Rs. 17,000 lakhs.

The initial margin charged by the broker is 10% of the contract value. On March 2, 2025, the Nifty futures contract closes at Rs. 22,150. What is the Mark-to-Market (MTM) gain for the trader on March 2, 2025?

**Options:**

A) Rs. 2,750
B) Rs. 3,750
C) Rs. 4,500
D) Rs. 5,000

**Answer:** Rs. 3,750

**Explanation:** MTM Gain = (Closing Price - Trade Price) * Lot Size = (22,150 - 22,000) * 25 = 150 * 25 = Rs. 3,750.

**Options:**

B) Rs. 100

**Question 7:** Case: An investor plans to buy 1,000 shares of XYZ Ltd. on July 15, 20XX, for which the current spot price is Rs. 500. To hedge against a potential price rise, the investor goes long one XYZ futures contract expiring on July 27, 20XX, at a price of Rs. 505. The lot size of the contract is 1,000 shares. On July 15, 20XX, the investor buys the shares in the spot market at Rs. 530, and the futures contract is trading at Rs. 532. What is the gain on the investor's futures position?

**Options:**

A) Rs. 25,000
B) Rs. 27,000
C) Rs. 30,000
D) Rs. 32,000

**Answer:** Rs. 27,000

**Explanation:** Gain on Futures Position = (Futures Sale Price - Futures Purchase Price) * Lot Size = (532 - 505) * 1,000 = 27 * 1,000 = Rs. 27,000.

**Question 8:** Case: An investor plans to buy 1,000 shares of XYZ Ltd. on July 15, 20XX, for which the current spot price is Rs. 500. To hedge against a potential price rise, the investor goes long one XYZ futures contract expiring on July 27, 20XX, at a price of Rs. 505. The lot size of the contract is 1,000 shares. On July 15, 20XX, the investor buys the shares in the spot market at Rs. 530, and the futures contract is trading at Rs. 532. What is the net effective purchase price per share for the investor?

**Options:**

A) Rs. 503
B) Rs. 505
C) Rs. 510
D) Rs. 520

**Answer:** Rs. 503

**Explanation:** Cost of Buying in Cash Market = 1,000 * 530 = Rs. 530,000. Net Cost = Cash Cost - Gain on Futures = 530,000 - 27,000 = Rs. 503,000. Net Cost per Share = 503,000 / 1,000 = Rs. 503.

**Question 10:** Case: The spot price of Stock B is Rs. 100. A one-month futures contract on Stock B is trading at Rs. 90. The contract multiplier is 200 shares. The arbitrageur can invest proceeds from cash market sales at 9% p.a. for one month. If the arbitrageur carries the position for one month, what is the total gain from this arbitrage, including interest earned, assuming continuous compounding?

**Options:**

A) Rs. 2,000
B) Rs. 2,150.56
C) Rs. 2,300
D) Rs. 2,450.56

**Answer:** Rs. 2,150.56

**Explanation:** Immediate gain = 200 * 10 = Rs. 2,000. Interest earned on Rs. 20,000 (100 * 200 shares) at 9% p.a. for one month (1/12 years) continuously compounded = 20,000 * (e^(0.09 * 1/12) - 1) = 20,000 * (e^0.0075 - 1) = 20,000 * (1.007528 - 1) = 20,000 * 0.007528 = Rs. 150.56. Total gain = 2,000 + 150.56 = Rs. 2,150.56.

To protect against a fall in price, the investor simultaneously buys a put option with a strike price of Rs. 1,600, paying a premium of Rs. 20. If the stock price rises to Rs. 1,660 at expiry, what is the net profit for the investor from this strategy?

**Options:**

A) Rs. 40
B) Rs. 50
C) Rs. 60
D) Rs. 80

**Answer:** Rs. 40

**Explanation:** Profit on Long Cash = 1,660 - 1,600 = 60. Loss on Long Put = -20 (since it expires worthless). Net Profit = 60 - 20 = Rs. 40.

**Question 18:** Case: A portfolio consists of three stocks: Stock X, Stock Y, and Stock Z, with respective betas of 0.8, 1.2, and 1.5. The investment proportions (weights) in the portfolio are 40% for Stock X, 30% for Stock Y, and 30% for Stock Z. The market value of the portfolio is Rs. 1,00,00,000. The index futures price is 20,000, and the lot size is 50. How many index futures contracts are required to hedge this portfolio's systematic risk (rounded to the nearest whole number)?

**Options:**

A) 8 contracts
B) 9 contracts
C) 10 contracts
D) 11 contracts

**Answer:** 11 contracts

**Explanation:** Number of contracts = (Value of Portfolio * Portfolio Beta) / (Index Futures Level * Contract Multiplier) = (1,00,00,000 * 1.07) / (20,000 * 50) = 1,07,00,000 / 10,00,000 = 10.7 contracts. Rounded to the nearest whole number, this is 11 contracts.
