**Question 1:** For order matching, the best buy order is the one with the:

**Options:**

A) Lowest price
B) Highest quantity
C) Highest price
D) Earliest time stamp

**Answer:** Highest price

**Explanation:** The system wants to match sellers with the highest possible prices offered by buyers, so the best buy order is the one with the highest bid price.

---

**Question 2:** For order matching, the best sell order is the one with the:

**Options:**

A) Highest price
B) Highest quantity
C) Lowest price
D) Latest time stamp

**Answer:** Lowest price

**Explanation:** The system wants to match buyers with the lowest possible prices offered by sellers, so the best sell order is the one with the lowest ask price.

**Question 4:** What is an order which is valid only for the single trading day on which it is entered?

**Options:**

A) IOC order
B) Limit order
C) Stop-loss order
D) Day order

**Answer:** Day order

**Explanation:** A Day order is automatically cancelled by the trading system if it is not executed by the end of the trading session.

**Question 5:** What type of order allows a user to buy or sell a contract immediately, with any unmatched portion automatically cancelled?

**Options:**

A) Day order
B) Limit order

D) Market order

**Answer:** IOC order

**Explanation:** An Immediate or Cancel (IOC) order requires execution immediately upon entering the system, either fully or partially, with the rest cancelled.

**Question 7:** What is an order to buy or sell a contract at the best bid/offer price currently available in the market?

**Question 8:** What type of order gets activated when a trigger price is reached or crossed, and then enters the market as a market or limit order?

**Question 9:** If the put open interest exceeds the call open interest (PCR > 1), it is typically taken as a:

**Options:**

A) Bearish signal
B) Bullish signal
C) Neutral signal
D) High volatility signal

**Answer:** Bullish signal

**Explanation:** A high PCR can suggest that more participants are betting on a price decline (buying puts) or selling puts, which could be interpreted as a bullish signal (selling puts implies expecting prices not to fall significantly).

---

**Question 10:** If the call open interest exceeds the put open interest (PCR < 1), it is typically taken as a:

**Options:**

A) Bearish signal
B) Bullish signal
C) Neutral signal
D) Low volatility signal

**Answer:** Bearish signal

**Explanation:** A low PCR can suggest that more participants are betting on a price increase (buying calls) or selling calls, which could be interpreted as a bearish signal (selling calls implies expecting prices not to rise significantly).

**Question 11:** What type of option spread involves options of the same type, having the same strike price, but different expiry dates?

**Question 13:** What strategy involves buying a call and a put option with the same strike prices and same maturity?

**Options:**

A) Strangle
B) Butterfly Spread
C) Long Straddle
D) Short Straddle

**Answer:** Long Straddle

**Explanation:** A long straddle is implemented by simultaneously purchasing a call and a put option on the same asset with identical strike prices and expiration dates.

**Question 14:** A long straddle strategy is undertaken when the trader expects:

**Options:**

A) The price of the underlying to remain stable
B) A significant movement in the price of the underlying in either direction
C) The price of the underlying to rise significantly
D) The price of the underlying to fall significantly

**Answer:** A significant movement in the price of the underlying in either direction

**Explanation:** This strategy profits from volatility, as it requires a large price move (either up or down) for the combined gain to exceed the cost of the premiums paid.

---

**Question 15:** What is the maximum loss for a long straddle position?

**Options:**

A) Unlimited
B) The strike price difference
C) The sum of the premiums paid
D) Zero

**Answer:** The sum of the premiums paid

**Explanation:** The worst-case scenario for a long straddle is when the underlying price finishes exactly at the strike price at expiry, resulting in both options expiring worthless.

---

**Question 16:** A long straddle position typically has how many break-even points (BEPs)?

**Options:**

A) One
B) Two
C) Three
D) Zero

**Answer:** Two

**Explanation:** A long straddle has an upper break-even point (Strike + Total Premium) and a lower break-even point (Strike - Total Premium).

**Question 17:** Which option among ITM, ATM, and OTM typically has the highest intrinsic value?

**Options:**

A) Deep OTM
B) ATM
C) Deep ITM
D) All have the same intrinsic value

**Answer:** Deep ITM

**Explanation:** Deep in-the-money options have the largest difference between the spot price and the strike price in the money-making direction.

**Question 18:** Which option among ITM, ATM, and OTM typically has only time value and no intrinsic value?

**Options:**

A) ITM

**Answer:** ATM and OTM

**Explanation:** Only In-the-money options have intrinsic value; At-the-money and Out-of-the-money options derive their value solely from time and expected volatility (time value).

**Question 22:** If a person is short a futures contract at Rs. 100 and the underlying price at expiry is Rs. 60, what is the payoff per unit?

**Options:**

A) -Rs. 40
B) Rs. 0
C) Rs. 40
D) Rs. 60

**Answer:** Rs. 40

**Explanation:** The seller is obligated to sell at 100. If the market price is 60, they can buy at 60 and sell at 100, gaining the difference (100 - 60).
