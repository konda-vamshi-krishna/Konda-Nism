# NISM Series VIII Equity Derivatives - Mock Test 4

**Question 1:** What happens to an exchange-traded option after its expiry date?

**Options:**

A) It can still be traded for two more days
B) It can be traded in the cash market
C) It cannot be traded
D) It converts into equity shares

**Answer:** It cannot be traded

**Explanation:** After expiry, the option contract ceases to exist. It cannot be traded once the expiry date is over.

---

**Question 2:** The premium paid by an option buyer remains with the exchange until the position is closed or expires. True or False?

**Options:**

A) True
B) False

**Answer:** False

**Explanation:** The exchange collects the premium, but it is transferred to the option seller. The exchange does not keep the premium.

---

**Question 3:** A call option is considered “In the Money” when:

**Options:**

A) Strike price is higher than spot price
B) Strike price is equal to spot price
C) Strike price is lower than spot price
D) None of the above

**Answer:** Strike price is lower than spot price

**Explanation:** A call option is profitable when the market price (spot) is above the strike price. That is called “In the Money.”

---

**Question 4:** In a CALL option contract, the buyer gets the right to:

**Options:**

A) Buy the underlying asset at a fixed price
B) Sell the underlying asset at a fixed price
C) Buy at market price only
D) Sell at market price only

**Answer:** Buy the underlying asset at a fixed price

**Explanation:** A call option gives the buyer the right, but not obligation, to buy the asset at a predetermined strike price.

---

**Question 5:** A put option is considered “Out of the Money” when:

**Options:**

A) Strike price is higher than spot price
B) Strike price is equal to spot price
C) Strike price is lower than spot price
D) None of the above

**Answer:** Strike price is lower than spot price

**Explanation:** A put option is profitable only when strike price is above spot price. If strike price is lower than spot price, it is out of the money.

**Options:**

A) A high-risk gamble
B) An insurance policy for your stocks
C) A bank loan
D) A lottery ticket

**Answer:** An insurance policy for your stocks

**Explanation:** It protects you from a market crash by giving you the right to sell at a fixed price.

**Question 91:** What is the "Put-Call Parity"?

**Options:**

A) A rule that puts must always be cheaper than calls
B) A mathematical balance between calls, puts, and the stock price
C) A law passed by the government
D) The number of buyers versus sellers

**Answer:** A mathematical balance between calls, puts, and the stock price

**Explanation:** If this balance is broken, an arbitrage opportunity exists.

**Question 92:** If the futures price is rising and Open Interest is ALSO rising, it indicates a:

**Options:**

A) Bullish trend
B) Bearish trend
C) Market crash
D) Holiday

**Answer:** Bullish trend

**Explanation:** Rising price + rising OI = new buyers entering (Bullish).
