**Question 1:** Which is an example of a calendar spread?

A) Buy and sell stock together
B) Buy one-month futures and sell another month futures

**Answer:** Buy one-month futures and sell another month futures

**Explanation:** Calendar spread involves buying and selling futures contracts of different expiry months.

**Question 2:** An index option is a __________________.(NISM workbook)

B) Derivative product

**Question 3:** The purchase of a share in one market and the simultaneous sale in a different market to benefit from price differentials is known as ____________.(NISM workbook)

**Options:**

A) Mortgage
B) Arbitrage
C) Hedging
D) Speculation

**Answer:** Arbitrage

**Explanation:** Arbitrage means buying in one market and simultaneously selling in another to profit from price differences. It is risk-free and exploits market inefficiencies.

**Question 6:** State whether TRUE or FALSE: Impact cost is low when the liquidity in the system is poor.(NISM workbook)

**Options:**

A) True
B) False

**Answer:** False

**Explanation:** Impact cost is inversely related to liquidity. When liquidity is poor, buying/selling large quantities moves prices more, making impact cost HIGH, not low.

**Question 7:** Which of the following costs is not actually paid by the market participants but arises due to lack of liquidity? (NISM workbook)

**Options:**

A) Securities Transaction Tax
B) Impact cost
C) SEBI charges
D) Brokerage

**Answer:** Impact cost

**Explanation:** Impact cost is an implicit cost — it is not paid directly but reflects the price movement caused by large orders in an illiquid market. STT and brokerage are explicit costs.

**Question 14:** You sold a Put option on a share. The strike price of the put was Rs. 245 and you received a premium of Rs. 49. Theoretically, what can be the maximum loss on this position?(NISM workbook)

**Question 16:** A European call option gives the buyer the right but not the obligation to buy from the seller an underlying at the prevailing market price "on or before" the expiry date.(NISM workbook)

**Options:**

**Question 17:** An in-the-money option is _____________.(NISM workbook)

**Options:**

A) An option with a negative intrinsic value
B) An option which cannot be profitably exercised by the holder immediately
C) An option with a positive intrinsic value
D) An option with zero-time value

**Answer:** An option with a positive intrinsic value

**Explanation:** An in-the-money option has positive intrinsic value — a call is ITM when market price > strike; a put is ITM when market price < strike.

**Question 18:** If an investor buys a call option with lower strike price and sells another call option with higher strike price, both on the same underlying share and same expiration date, the strategy is called ___________(NISM workbook)

**Options:**

A) Bullish spread
B) Bearish spread
C) Butterfly spread
D) Calendar spread

**Answer:** Bullish spread

**Explanation:** Buying a lower-strike call and selling a higher-strike call on the same expiry creates a Bull Call Spread (Bullish spread), profiting when the underlying price rises.

**Question 19:** Which of the following is a hedged position?(NISM workbook)

**Options:**

A) Short straddle
B) Short strangle
C) Covered call
D) Protective put

**Answer:** Protective put

**Explanation:** A protective put involves holding a stock and buying a put option, which hedges downside risk. Covered call provides partial hedge; short straddle/strangle are unhedged strategies.

**Question 23:** A trader has bought 100 shares of XYZ at Rs 780 per share. He expects the price to go up but wants to protect himself if the price falls. He does not want to lose more than Rs. 1000 on this long position in XYZ. What should the trader do?(NISM workbook)

**Options:**

C) Place a limit buy order for 100 shares of XYZ at Rs 790 per share
D) Place a limit buy order for 100 shares of XYZ at Rs 770 per share

**Question 24:** Trader A wants to sell 20 contracts of August series at Rs 4500 and Trader B wants to sell 17 contracts of September series at Rs 4550. Lot size is 50. The Initial Margin is fixed at 6%. How much Initial Margin is required from both investors combined? (NISM workbook)

**Options:**

A) 2,70,000
B) 5,02,050
C) 2,32,050

**Answer:** 5,02,050

**Explanation:** A's margin = 20 x 50 x 4500 x 6% = Rs 2,70,000. B's margin = 17 x 50 x 4550 x 6% = Rs 2,32,050. Total = 2,70,000 + 2,32,050 = Rs 5,02,050.
